What is Boxing?

Boxing is a combat sport in which two participants, wearing padded gloves, exchange punches inside a roped ring over timed rounds. It combines speed, power, footwork, and ring IQ.

Why Boxing Matters

Beyond physical prowess, boxing builds discipline, mental toughness, and confidence. Legends like Muhammad Ali transcended the sport to inspire social change.

Boxing Styles

  • Out-Boxer: Uses reach and speed to score. (e.g., Floyd Mayweather)
  • Swarmer: Pressures opponents at close range. (e.g., Joe Frazier)
  • Slugger: Relies on power and knockouts. (e.g., George Foreman)
  • Counterpuncher: Waits for mistakes to strike. (e.g., Juan Manuel MÃ¡rquez)

Benefits of Boxing

  • Cardiovascular health and strength
  • Improved coordination and reflexes
  • Mental resilience and focus
  • Stress relief and self-defense skills
